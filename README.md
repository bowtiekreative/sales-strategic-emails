# Sales & Strategic Emails — The Knowledge Brain 🧠🎯

**AI agent skills for sales emails, proposals, negotiation, and conflict resolution**

A curated collection of **12 interlinked methodologies** — each is a Hermes Agent skill (`SKILL.md` with YAML frontmatter) that any AI agent can load instantly. But this isn't just a list. It's a **knowledge graph** — every skill is mapped to the scenarios it dominates, the people it connects to, and the moments it should (or should not) be used.

## 🗺️ SCENARIO MAP — What to Use When

> *Facing a situation? Find it below, grab the right method.*

### 📧 Outreach / Prospecting

| Scenario | Primary Skill | Secondary | Why |
|----------|--------------|-----------|-----|
| **Cold email to a stranger** | Gary Vee | SPIN Selling | Jab jab jab — give value before asking. SPIN's Situation questions warm them up |
| **Warm follow-up (met at event)** | Solutions Selling | Xerox | Diagnose their problem from the conversation — prescribe a solution |
| **Re-engaging a cold lead** | Chris Voss | Yin Yang | "It's completely fine if you've moved on" — Voss's late-night FM turn |
| **Referral request** | Tony Robbins RPM | Grant Cardone | Frame it as a mission, then 10X the ask |

### 🤝 Discovery / Qualification

| Scenario | Primary Skill | Secondary | Why |
|----------|--------------|-----------|-----|
| **First discovery call** | Xerox Consultative | SPIN Selling | Xerox's 7-step is the blueprint. SPIN adds structured questioning |
| **Multi-stakeholder sale** | SPIN Selling | Solutions Selling | Implication questions make each stakeholder feel their specific pain |
| **Budget unknown** | Chris Voss | SPIN | "What budget were you thinking?" — calibrated open-ended questions |
| **They don't know their problem** | Solutions Selling | Xerox | Diagnose before prescribing — classic needs analysis |

### 🗣️ Objections / Pushback

| Scenario | Primary Skill | Secondary | Why |
|----------|--------------|-----------|-----|
| **"It's too expensive"** | Chris Voss | Yin Yang | Mirror them: "Too expensive?" then label — "Sounds like this isn't the right fit" |
| **"We're happy with X"** | Best Block Not to Be There | Art of War | Don't attack the competitor. Sidestep entirely: "That makes sense. What would need to change for you to look?" |
| **"Not the right time"** | Jordan Belfort | Gary Vee | Belfort: create urgency. Vee: respects timing while staying top-of-mind |
| **"We need to think about it"** | Grant Cardone (10X) | Chris Voss | Selina close: "What specifically gives you hesitation?" or Voss: "What do you need to hear to say yes?" |
| **Silence / ghosting** | Best Block | Gary Vee | The best block is not to push. Vee: keep jabbing with value |
| **Vague objection ("I'm not sure")** | SPIN Selling | Xerox | Dig into Implication — "What happens if you don't solve this?" |

### 💰 Closing

| Scenario | Primary Skill | Secondary | Why |
|----------|--------------|-----------|-----|
| **Negotiating price down** | Chris Voss (Ackerman) | Art of War | Ackerman's 65-85-95-100 system. Art of War: know their walkaway point |
| **Urgent same-day close** | Jordan Belfort | Grant Cardone | Straight Line energy + 10X closing. Time-pressure + tonality |
| **High-ticket enterprise deal** | Solutions Selling | SPIN | ROI-heavy. Let them sell themselves on the numbers |
| **Competitive bidding** | Art of War | Yin Yang | Know their offering better than they do. Flip their strength into your entry point |
| **Objection-laden tough close** | Chris Voss | Jordan Belfort | Tactical empathy first. If that stalls, Straight Line tonality to reset |

### 😡 Conflict / Complaints / Recovery

| Scenario | Primary Skill | Secondary | Why |
|----------|--------------|-----------|-----|
| **Client complaint** | Conflict Resolution | Chris Voss | De-escalate → understand → reframe → resolve. Voss's labeling calms the amygdala |
| **Partnership dispute** | Conflict Resolution | Art of War | Know their position. Win without fighting — find the aligned outcome |
| **Internal team conflict** | Tony Robbins RPM | Conflict Resolution | 6 Human Needs: what need is unmet? Frame resolution around that need |
| **Customer churn / at-risk account** | Chris Voss | Solutions Selling | "Help me understand" + re-diagnose the disconnect |
| **Difficult email response** | Best Block | Yin Yang | Respond to the intent, not the tone. Flip hostility into opportunity |

### 📝 Proposal Writing

| Scenario | Primary Skill | Secondary | Why |
|----------|--------------|-----------|-----|
| **Competitive proposal** | Art of War | Solutions Selling | Structure it around their vulnerabilities, not your features |
| **ROI-driven proposal** | Solutions Selling | SPIN | Hard numbers + Implication → Need-Payoff arc |
| **Simple proposal** | Xerox Consultative | Gary Vee | FAB framework (Features → Advantages → Benefits) + authenticity |
| **Partnership proposal** | Chris Voss | Tony Robbins | Mirror their language. Frame as shared mission (RPM) |

## ⚖️ The Tension Map — When Methods Clash

> *Not every approach works together. Here's how to choose.*

```
AGGRESSIVE ←────────────────────────────→ PATIENT

    Belfort · Cardone          Vee · Voss · Best Block
    (Force urgency)            (Let the deal breathe)

LOGICAL ←──────────────────────────────→ PSYCHOLOGICAL

    SPIN · Xerox · Solutions   Voss · Robbins · Yin Yang
    (Structured process)       (Emotion & state first)

CONFRONT ←──────────────────────────────→ DEFLECT

    Belfort · Cardone · AoW    Best Block · Yin Yang · Voss
    (Attack objection)         (Redirect / reframe)

SHORT GAME ←──────────────────────────────→ LONG GAME

    Belfort · Cardone             Vee · SPIN · Solutions
    (Close now)                   (Build over time)
```

### 🚫 Anti-Patterns — What to NEVER Combine

| Don't Use A | With B | Why It Breaks |
|-------------|--------|---------------|
| **Belfort** | **Best Block** | Belfort attacks. Best Block sidesteps. You'll sound confused. |
| **Cardone 10X** | **Vee patience** | "I'm calling 10 times" + "I'll jab for 6 months" = contradictory energy. Pick one per email. |
| **Chris Voss** | **Cardone Selina Close** | Voss's "That doesn't work for me" demos weakness. Cardone's Selina forces assumption of yes. They cancel out. |
| **Best Block** | **SPIN Implication** | Best Block wants to avoid pain. SPIN Implication wants to amplify pain. Choose: deflect or dig? |
| **Art of War** | **Gary Vee** | AoW is about strategic positioning over the enemy. Vee says there is no enemy. If you're in a competitive battle, use AoW. If you're building community, use Vee. Mixing them feels schizophrenic. |

## 🧠 The Meta-Pattern

All 12 skills reduce to **three core questions**:

1. **Who are they** (what do they need, fear, want)? → Art of War, Xerox, SPIN, Vee
2. **Where am I** (position, strength, weakness)? → Yin Yang, Best Block, Robbins
3. **How do I move us forward** (what's the next inch)? → Voss, Belfort, Cardone, Conflict Resolution

Master these three, and you don't need the tree. The tree is just training wheels.
