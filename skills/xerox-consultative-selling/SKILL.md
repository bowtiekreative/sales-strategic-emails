# Xerox Consultative Selling

The original solution-selling methodology — developed by Xerox in the 1970s to sell complex office systems. It's the foundation that modern frameworks like SPIN and Solution Selling built on.

> **Core mantra:** "The prospect doesn't care about your product. They care about their problem. Help them solve it, and you'll earn the sale."

---

## 🔑 Core Principles

### 1. The 7-Step Xerox Sales Cycle

| Step | Name | Purpose |
|------|------|---------|
| 1 | **Prospecting** | Find qualified leads |
| 2 | **Pre-Approach** | Research and prepare |
| 3 | **Approach** | Build rapport, establish credibility |
| 4 | **Needs Development** | Ask questions and listen (THE most important step) |
| 5 | **Presentation** | Present solution to the needs, not the features |
| 6 | **Objections** | Address concerns constructively |
| 7 | **Close** | Get commitment |

### 2. Needs Development (Step 4 — The Heart)
Xerox taught that **80% of a sales call should be listening.** The key is the **SPIN precursor** — open-ended questions that develop the need.

**Question types:**
- **Fact-finding:** "Walk me through your current process..."
- **Feeling questions:** "How satisfied are you with [current solution]?"
- **Problem identification:** "What's the biggest frustration you deal with?"

**The 80/20 Rule:** Listen 80% of the time, talk 20%. The 20% should be questions and summaries.

### 3. The FAB Framework (Feature-Advantage-Benefit)

| Level | Definition | Example |
|-------|------------|---------|
| **Feature** | What it is | "20 pages per minute" |
| **Advantage** | What it does | "Prints faster" |
| **Benefit** | What it means to THEM | "Your team spends less time waiting, more time selling" |

A feature is nothing. A benefit tied to their specific need is everything.

### 4. Active Listening Triad
- **Paraphrase:** "So if I understand correctly, you're saying that..."
- **Clarify:** "When you say [term], what do you mean specifically?"
- **Confirm:** "Does that sound right?"

### 5. The Opening Statement Pattern
```
"I understand from my research that [acknowledge their situation].
Some people in your position struggle with [common pain].
What's it been like for you?"
```

### 6. Objection Handling — The Feel-Felt-Found Method
- **Feel:** "I understand how you feel..."
- **Felt:** "Other clients have felt the same way..."
- **Found:** "What they found was that..."

## 📝 Email Templates

### Needs Development — The Discovery Email
```
Subject: [Number] quick questions

Hi [Name],

I want to make sure I understand your situation before I suggest anything. Help me fill in the blanks:

1. How are you currently handling [specific function]?
2. What's working well about it?
3. If you could change one thing, what would it be?

Three questions. No pitch. Just want to hear from you.

Best,
[Your name]
```

### The FAB Proposal Email
```
Hi [Name],

Based on our conversation, here's what I'd recommend:

The challenge you described: [their specific need]

Here's how we address it:
[Feature] → which means [advantage] → so that you get [benefit specific to them]

[Feature] → which means [advantage] → so that you get [benefit specific to them]

[Feature] → which means [advantage] → so that you get [benefit specific to them]

Three benefits, each tied directly to what you told me matters most.

Worth a conversation to walk through the details?
```

### Objection Handling — Feel-Felt-Found
```
Subject: You're not alone on this

Hi [Name],

I hear your concern about [objection]. Honestly, I'd be worried if you weren't thinking about it.

Several of our best clients felt the exact same way before they started working with us.

What they found was [outcome that disproves the objection without arguing].

I'm not saying it's for everyone — but I'd hate for [objection] to be the reason you don't explore a solution that could [key benefit].

Want to talk it through?
```

## ⚠️ Pitfalls

| Mistake | Why It Fails |
|---------|-------------|
| Talking too much | You learn nothing when you're talking. 80/20 rule exists for a reason |
| Premature presentation | If you present before fully understanding the need, you'll miss the mark |
| Feature dumping | Features without benefits tied to their need = noise |
| Ignoring their feelings | The Xerox model is built on empathy — skip it and lose trust |
| Weak close | Asking "Do you want to buy?" is amateur. "Based on what you've shared..." closes naturally |

## 🎯 When to Use

**Trigger conditions:**
- Enterprise B2B sales
- First-time discovery calls
- Selling to C-suite or senior decision makers
- Complex solutions with multiple components
- Need to establish trust before talking product
- Long-cycle relationship selling

**Do NOT use when:**
- Transactional, self-service sale
- The buyer already knows what they want
- Low-value, one-call close
