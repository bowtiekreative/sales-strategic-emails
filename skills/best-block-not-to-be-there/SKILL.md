# The Best Block Is Not to Be There

Derived from martial arts philosophy (tai chi, Bruce Lee's *Jeet Kune Do*, and Sun Tzu) — **the highest skill is not fighting the battle at all.** Applied to sales: the best objection handle is to not be where the objection lands. The best competitor match is to not play their game.

> **Core mantra:** "Be water, my friend." — Bruce Lee

---

## 🔑 Core Principles

### 1. Redirection Over Resistance
Never meet force with force. If a prospect pushes back, don't push harder — **step aside and let their momentum carry them past you**, then re-enter from a different angle.

- **Objection:** "Your price is too high"
- **Bad block:** "Let me justify the price with these features"
- **No-block:** *"You're right — it is more than the alternative. I'd be worried if you didn't question it. What specifically are you comparing it to?"*

### 2. The Sidestep (Instead of the Parry)
When asked a question that boxes you in, don't answer directly. Answer the question they *should* have asked.

- **They ask:** "How does this compare to [competitor]?"
- **Sidestep:** *"Great question. Let me tell you what our clients tell us they value most, and you can judge for yourself."*
- **They ask:** "Can you give me a discount?"
- **Sidestep:** *"Let me show you what that discount would cost you in [value area]. If after seeing that you still want it, let's talk."*

### 3. Absence as Leverage
Not being available creates scarcity. Not rushing creates value. Not chasing creates attraction.

- **In follow-ups:** Wait an extra day longer than you think you should
- **In negotiation:** Silence is a weapon — after making an offer, shut up
- **In proposals:** Leave something on the table *unsaid* — let them ask for it
- **In positioning:** Don't be everywhere the competitor is — be where they aren't

### 4. The Empty Space Strategy
> *"The value of a room is in the empty space, not the walls."*

- In your pitch, leave gaps for the prospect to fill with their own reasoning
- Don't over-explain. State the value and let their mind connect the dots
- A proposal that answers every question leaves no room for their ownership

### 5. Flow Like Water
- **Adaptability:** If one angle doesn't work, flow around the obstacle, don't crash into it
- **Soft entry:** Start conversations low-stakes. Let the prospect pull information, don't push it
- **Surround, don't attack:** Build value from multiple angles (social proof, case studies, ROI data) rather than one frontal argument

### 6. The Principle of Least Action
The path of least resistance wins. Remove friction instead of adding force.

- Instead of convincing, **remove objections piece by piece**
- Instead of closing hard, **make buying easy**
- Instead of proving you're right, **make them discover it**

## 📝 Email Templates

### Objection Redirection — "Step Aside"
```
Subject: You're not wrong

Hi [Name],

You're right that [their objection]. If I were in your shoes, I'd be asking the same questions.

Let me ask you this instead:

[Redirection question that reframes the value]

What would it take for [the objection] to no longer be a dealbreaker?

Happy to have that conversation.
```

### The Sidestep — Avoiding a Price Comparison
```
Thanks for the comparison request. Honestly, it's apples and oranges — and I'd rather be straight with you than pretend otherwise.

What [Competitor] does well is [their strength]. What we do differently is [our strength].

The real question isn't which is better on paper — it's which one solves the specific problem you're facing right now.

Let me ask: what's the #1 thing you need this to fix?
```

### Absence as Leverage — Slow Follow-Up
```
Subject: No rush

Hi [Name],

I know you have a lot on your plate. Figured I'd check in without adding pressure.

If the timing's not right, it's not right. If something changed, no hard feelings.

Just wanted you to know the offer is still on the table — but not forever. Let me know when you're ready to talk.

Best,
[Your name]
```

### The Empty Space — Partial Proposal
```
Hi [Name],

Here's what I've put together so far based on our conversation:

[Brief value summary — 3 bullet points max]

One thing I intentionally left out — I want to hear your thoughts on what you need most right now before I finalize the scope.

What's the #1 outcome you're looking for in the next 30 days?
```

## ⚠️ Pitfalls

| Mistake | Why It Fails |
|---------|-------------|
| Passive-aggressive silence | Absence without warmth feels like neglect — still need to add value |
| Sidestepping important questions | You're being evasive, not strategic. Core questions need direct answers |
| Over-doing the "cool" act | Too much detachment looks like you don't care |
| Using it in low-trust situations | This works when trust exists; without trust, sidestepping feels manipulative |
| Never closing the gap | Flow must eventually converge — at some point you need a commitment |

---

## 🎯 When to Use

**Trigger conditions:**
- Prospect is defensive or combative
- You're in a price war you can't win on features
- Competitor has a stronger feature set
- Prospect asks "gotcha" questions
- You need to slow down a deal that's moving too fast
- The relationship needs more trust before closing

**Do NOT use when:**
- Direct, transparent communication is expected (audits, compliance)
- The prospect needs straightforward answers
- You've already established trust and need to move to close
- The deal is purely transactional with low relationship value
