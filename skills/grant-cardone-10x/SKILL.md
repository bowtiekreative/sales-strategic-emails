# Grant Cardone (10X)

Grant Cardone's **10X Rule** and **The Closer's Survival Guide** — the philosophy of taking massive action to close more deals, dominate your market, and never accept "no" as final.

> **Core mantra:** "You must take massive action. Average action equals average results."

---

## 🔑 Core Principles

### 1. The 10X Rule
Whatever you think you need to do — **do 10 times more.**
- 10X the activity (calls, emails, proposals)
- 10X the follow-up (most sales happen after the 5th contact)
- 10X the belief (if you don't believe, nobody else will)
- 10X the commitment (partial effort = partial results)

### 2. Massive Action ≠ Action
Most people take *some* action. The market rewards *massive* action.

| Level | Description | Result |
|-------|-------------|--------|
| **Average** | Do what's expected | Average results, average income |
| **10X** | Do what seems unreasonable | Breakthrough results |
| **Widespread** | Multiple channels, all at once | Market dominance |

### 3. The Selina Closing Framework
Named after Cardone's wife — a high-empathy, high-desire close built on **future-pacing**:

1. **Ask them about their dream** (the car, the house, the lifestyle)
2. **Agree with everything they want** (validation)
3. **Connect** their dream = more sales = more commission = faster lifestyle
4. **Close with logic:** *"Do you want this or not?"*

**The Selina Close Script:**
> "I want something from you. I want you to be honest with me. Can you do that?
> What's a meaningful amount of money to you? [Amount].
> Great. That's how much this deal would be worth to me.
> Now tell me — what do you want to do?"

### 4. The Follow-Up System

| Contact # | When | Type |
|-----------|------|------|
| 1 | Day 1 | Initial contact |
| 2 | Day 3 | Value-add (article, insight) |
| 3 | Day 5 | Check-in, "thinking of you" |
| 4 | Day 8 | Case study / social proof |
| 5 | Day 12 | "I'm not giving up on you" |
| 6 | Day 18 | New development / incentive |
| 7 | Day 25 | The "decision time" call |
| 8 | Day 35 | The "one last thing" email |
| 9 | Day 50 | Personal handwritten note |
| 10 | Day 75 | "Been quiet — what changed?" |

**Cardone says:** 80% of sales happen between the 5th and 10th contact, but 80% of salespeople stop after the 3rd contact. **You are the persistence they can't outlast.**

### 5. The 5 Rules of Closing

1. **Always be closing** — every interaction moves forward
2. **Ask for the order multiple times** — the first "no" is rarely final
3. **Close on objections** — every objection is a buying signal
4. **Use the "assumption close"** — act like they've already decided
5. **Create urgency** — if there's no hurry, there's no deal

### 6. The "How Bad Do You Want It?" Close
```
"You can [achieve the outcome] — or you can stay where you are.
The difference is $[price].
That's [coffee/day] for the next [timeframe].
Do you want this or not?"

Don't answer for them. Let silence hang.
```

## 📝 Email Templates

### The Follow-Up That Won't Quit (Contact #4)
```
Subject: Thought you'd appreciate this

Hi [Name],

No pitch. Just found this case study of [similar company] who was dealing with [their similar problem].

They solved it with [approach] and got [specific result]. Sound familiar?

Just sharing because I think about your situation when I see things like this.

No need to reply.
```

### The "Decision Time" Email (Contact #7)
```
Subject: It's decision time

[Name],

We've had [number] conversations over the past [timeframe]. You've seen the numbers, the case studies, and the proof.

The offer I put together expires on [date]. Not because I'm trying to be difficult — because the terms I got for you are time-bound.

Here's what I need from you: a yes or a no.

If the answer is no — cool. Tell me why and we'll figure it out.
But if the answer is yes — let's get it done today.

What's it going to be?
```

### Assumption Close Email (Act Like They Said Yes)
```
Subject: Next steps

[Name],

Following up on our conversation — I've gone ahead and prepared your [onboarding / setup / welcome kit].

Here's what happens next:
1. [First step]
2. [Second step]
3. [Third step]

Take a look and let me know if everything looks good. I'll have the team ready to start on [date].

Looking forward to getting this going.
```

## ⚠️ Pitfalls

| Mistake | Why It Fails |
|---------|-------------|
| Being annoying not persistent | There's a fine line — add value every time, not pressure |
| 10X without focus | Spraying 10X effort in every direction = burnout, not results |
| Too much assumption | Pushing ahead as if they said yes without reading the room = arrogant |
| Forgetting to nurture | 10X is volume AND value — value must scale with the volume |
| Not disqualifying | Sometimes no means no — know when to move on |

---

## 🎯 When to Use

**Trigger conditions:**
- High-volume sales (100+ prospects in pipeline)
- Long-cycle deals that need nurturing
- Prospects who are slow to decide
- Sales environments with high attrition
- You need to dominate a specific market/territory
- The competitor is outselling you on volume alone

**Do NOT use when:**
- The prospect has explicitly asked to be left alone
- The relationship is fragile and needs space
- Compliance/regulation limits contact frequency
- It's a referral-based sale (too much pressure backfires)
