# Gary Vee (Jab Jab Jab Right Hook)

Gary Vaynerchuk's philosophy: **Give, give, give — then ask.** Build trust and goodwill through relentless value delivery before you ever ask for the order. When you do ask, make it count.

> **Core mantra:** "The best time to plant a tree was 20 years ago. The second best time is now. Start giving value today, so you can ask tomorrow."

---

## 🔑 Core Principles

### 1. The Jab Jab Jab Right Hook Framework

| Round | Action | Purpose |
|-------|--------|---------|
| **Jab** | Give value — no ask | Build trust, demonstrate expertise |
| **Jab** | Give more value — still no ask | Establish relationship, create reciprocity |
| **Jab** | Give even more value — personal touch | Deepen connection, understand their needs |
| **Right Hook** | Ask for the sale | Convert goodwill into business |

**The ratio:** For every 1 ask (right hook), deliver 10-15 jabs (value without ask).

### 2. The Gratitude Marketing Framework
Gary Vee's most underrated principle: **gratitude sells.**

- Thank your customers publicly and specifically
- Appreciate their business before asking for more
- Gratitude emails have higher open rates than sales emails
- "Thank you for your time" — but mean it, don't just say it

### 3. The $1.80 Rule of Attention
In a world where everyone is trying to sell at $1.80, be the one who **gives** $1.80 of value first.

- Your first email should be 100% value, 0% ask
- Your first meeting should be about them, not you
- Your first follow-up should be something useful, not "just checking in"

### 4. Content-Based Selling
> *"Document, don't create."*

- Share real stories of real results (with permission)
- Screen-record quick walkthroughs for individual prospects
- Write up insights from your industry — become their trusted source
- The more value you give away, the more they'll want to pay for the full version

### 5. Patience in the Long Game
- McDonald's customer profile matters more than the individual sale
- **$1 today from someone who stays for 10 years = $10+++**
- Short-term aggressive closing kills long-term relationship value
- Content marketing is compound interest — slow start, exponential later

### 6. The Reverse Ask
Sometimes the most powerful right hook is actually **not asking.**

- "I don't think you're ready for this yet. Let me send you some resources."
- "Actually, I'd recommend you start with [smaller commitment] first."
- "This might not be the right fit. Let me suggest something else."

The reverse ask builds trust that pays off when they're truly ready.

## 📝 Email Templates

### The Pure Jab — No Ask, All Value
```
Subject: Something I thought you'd find useful

[Name],

Stumbled across this [article / tool / insight] and immediately thought of you and [their situation].

[Valuable content — a PDF, a template, a how-to guide, a market insight]

No strings. Just wanted to share something I thought would help.

Hope it's useful.
```

### The Gratitude Jab
```
Subject: Thank you

Hi [Name],

Quick note to say thank you for [specific thing they did — taking a call, referring someone, being a client, giving feedback].

I know your time is valuable, and I don't take it for granted.

Because of clients like you, I've been able to [specific improvement you made based on client feedback].

If there's ever anything I can do to help, just say the word.

Sincerely,
[Your name]
```

### The Right Hook (After 10-15 Jabs)
```
Subject: An idea for you

Hi [Name],

We've been in touch for [timeframe], so I feel like I know your situation pretty well.

Based on everything I've seen, I think now might be the right time to [specific action].

Here's why:
- [Reason 1 tied to their specific situation]
- [Reason 2 tied to a recent development]
- [Reason 3 — the opportunity cost of waiting]

I wouldn't ask if I didn't genuinely believe it was the right move.

Want to explore it?
```

### The Reverse Ask (Permission Marketing)
```
Subject: I don't think you're ready — and that's ok

Hi [Name],

I've been thinking about our last conversation.

Honestly? I don't think [my solution] is the right next step for you right now.

Here's what I'd recommend instead:
[Smaller commitment / free resource / alternative approach]

When you're ready for the full solution, I'll be here. But I'd rather see you succeed with a smaller step than fail with the big one.

Let me know if you want to start there.
```

## ⚠️ Pitfalls

| Mistake | Why It Fails |
|---------|-------------|
| Jabbing forever, never hooking | Value without ask is charity, not sales |
| Hook too early | If you ask before the jabs pile up, you broke the trust you hadn't earned |
| Jabs feel fake | If your value-give doesn't feel genuine, it's worse than asking directly |
| No patience | This method works on long cycles — impatient people give up too soon |
| Forgetting the ask entirely | Gratefulness without conversion = you're a free consultant |

## 🎯 When to Use

**Trigger conditions:**
- Long-cycle relationship sales
- Content marketing and inbound leads
- High-value, high-trust solutions
- Selling to an audience that follows you
- Prospect is not yet ready to buy
- Retaining and upselling existing clients
- Building a personal brand alongside sales

**Do NOT use when:**
- Transactional, one-off sale (waste of time)
- Urgent close needed
- Prospect has already decided and wants to buy now (just take the order!)
- Selling to price-shoppers who don't value relationships
